Projects

My thesis work has focused on machine learning in chemical engineering applications.

Machine Learning for Manufacturing

I am generally interested in data-driven modeling for complex systems. I have applied machine learning methods in critical quality attribute prediction for biopharmaceutical manufacturing, anomaly detection for production oil and gas wells, and capacity modeling for lithium ion batteries.

Missing Data

One of my focus areas is methods that can be applied to datasets with missing values. Missing data is a common problem and may be related to sample handling, changes in sensor systems, or values that are outside of the sensor detection limit. To address these issues in the context of classification modeling, I have proposed an algorithm that results in a sparse linear model. The approach was tested on high-dimensional biological datasets and is publicly available.

Also in the area of missing data, we recently published a tutorial paper on PCA methods for missing data. In concert with this, I have provided code to add missingess to datasets for method development.

Machine Learning and Healthcare

Outside of my thesis work, I am interested in machine learning in healthcare. My interest in this topic started in an MIT class on secondary analysis of electronic healthcare records.
